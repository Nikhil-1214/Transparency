Parliamentary Structure

India follows a bicameral legislature system.

Lok Sabha

The House of the People (Lower House). Members are directly elected by the citizens of India.

  • Max Strength: 550 members
  • Term: 5 Years
  • Election: Universal Adult Suffrage

Rajya Sabha

The Council of States (Upper House). Members are elected by the elected members of State Legislative Assemblies.

  • Max Strength: 250 members
  • Term: 6 Years (Permanent Body)
  • 1/3rd members retire every 2 years

The Election Process

Election Commission of India (ECI)

An autonomous constitutional authority responsible for administering election processes in India.

EVM & VVPAT

Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) are used to cast votes. VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail) allows voters to verify their vote was cast correctly.

Indelible Ink

Applied to the voter's finger to prevent double voting, a hallmark of Indian elections.

How to Vote?

  1. Check Registration

    Ensure your name is on the electoral roll via the ECI website.

  2. Visit Polling Booth

    Carry your Voter ID (EPIC) or valid ID proof.

  3. Cast Vote

    Press the button on the EVM against your chosen candidate.
